The British Rail Class 80x is a modern high-speed train built as part of the United Kingdom’s Intercity Express Programme. Designed and manufactured by Hitachi Rail, these trains are based on the Hitachi AT300 platform and are used on long-distance routes across Britain.

Class 80x trains are designed for flexibility. Many versions are bi-mode, meaning they can run using electric power from overhead wires or switch to onboard diesel engines when electrification is not available. This allows them to operate on both modern electrified lines and older railway routes without needing a locomotive change.

Inside the train, passengers typically find modern seating, power outlets, Wi-Fi connectivity, and quieter ride quality compared with older rolling stock. Large windows and improved lighting also help create a more comfortable environment for long journeys.

Today, Class 80x trains form the backbone of several major intercity fleets in the UK, helping replace older high-speed trains while maintaining fast and reliable services between major cities.
